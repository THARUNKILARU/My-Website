About

I make healthcare data
move correctly.

I'm Tharun Kilaru — a Senior EDI Specialist and developer at International Benefits Administrators (IBA), a Third Party Administrator in Garden City, NY. For 4+ years I've been building and maintaining the data pipelines that connect employers, carriers, and benefit administrators in the US healthcare system.

Before IBA, I worked at UnitedHealth Group processing high-volume 837 claims, and before that in retail and logistics EDI at PetSmart and APL Logistics. That breadth means I understand EDI as a universal data standard, not just one industry's quirk.

At IBA I'm essentially the entire EDI engineering team — I build the tools, write the automations, onboard trading partners, and fix things when they break. I write about that work publicly because most EDI resources are too shallow or locked behind paywalls. These are working notes, made public.

How I Work

Approach & Values

⚙️ Automate the repetitive
Any task I do more than twice on a fixed schedule gets automated. Manual work introduces error. Automation doesn't forget, doesn't typo, doesn't miss a Friday.
🔍 Fail loudly
A silent failure is worse than no automation at all. Every system I build has error alerting. If something breaks, I know within minutes, not days.
📝 Document everything
A task I built six months ago looks foreign when I open it cold. Comments, notes, and runbooks aren't overhead — they're how you work at speed sustainably.
🌐 Stay ahead of the spec
Healthcare interoperability is changing — FHIR, CMS mandates, prior auth modernization. I learn the new while mastering the existing. Both matter.
